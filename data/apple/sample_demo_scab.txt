Brief for the grower:
Apple scab (Venturia inaequalis) shows olive-green spots on leaves and fruit, darkening and cracking over time. In the south, risk is higher in humid spring periods. Prevention: collect and destroy fallen leaves, thin the canopy for airflow.

Main findings:
First signs appear on young leaves in spring. On fruit, spots are corky and reduce market quality. Integrated measures: variety choice, spray timing by phenology, weather awareness. In high humidity, intervals between treatments are shortened.

Figures from the text:
- Temperature 10–16 °C and rain favor ascospore infection.
- Incubation from infection to visible spots — about 9–14 days in warm weather.
